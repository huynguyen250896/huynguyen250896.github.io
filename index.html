About Me

Who I Am?

Hi! I am Huy. I am a 1st-year 2nd-year 3rd-year PhD student in Computer Science at Auburn University, where I work in the Data Science and Engineering Lab under the supervision of Prof. Wei-Shinn (Jeff) Ku. I have also been fortunate to receive mentorship at Auburn from Dr. Phi Hung Bya, Dr. Zongliang (Wells) Yue, Prof. Hao Chen, and Prof. Jiaqi (Jack) Wang. Prior to my PhD, I spent two wonderful years at VinBigData as a Research Scientist, where I was supervised by Prof. Duc-Hau Le and Dr. Nam S. Vo. During that time, I was a core member of the VN1K, the largest genome project in Vietnam, which focused on building a comprehensive database of genomic variants for the Vietnamese population.

I'm always open to collaborations, discussions, and new opportunities. Feel free to reach out if you're interested in my research or would like to discuss potential projects.

Research: My primary research focuses on:
1. Computational Biology. My work spans multi-omics studies integrating genomics, transcriptomics, metabolomics, and proteomics. This includes bulk, single-cell, and spatially resolved analyses, pathway analysis, variant calling (both germline and somatic), genome-wide association studies (GWAS), and polygenic risk scores (PRS).
2. Computational Healthcare Informatics. I develop and apply data-driven methods for learning from heterogeneous clinical and biological data to support decision-making in medicine and public health.
I also address core AI challenges raised by these problems, including multi-modal modeling, agentic reasoning, uncertainty quantification, federated and collaborative learning, etc.
